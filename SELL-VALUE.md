# Sell Value & Prices — What the Numbers Mean

The app's original job: show your **duplicate** cards and **what you'd actually get** for selling
the extras. Here's how that number is worked out.

## Two ways to sell a card
1. **Sell to highest buy order** (the market) — someone has a standing **buy order**; you sell into
   it instantly at that price. The marketplace takes a **10% tax**, so you pocket **90%**.
   - Example: highest buy order is **10,000** stubs → you receive **9,000** after tax.
2. **Quick sell** — a **fixed** value set by the card's OVR/rarity, paid instantly, **no tax** and no
   buyer needed. It's the floor. (For non-Live cards it's **50%** of the Live quick-sell value.)

## Which one the app shows
For each card the app compares the two and shows the **better net payout**, labeled with the method:
- **"sell to highest"** when a buy order beats quick-sell (the amount shown is already **after the 10% tax**), or
- **"quick sell"** when there's no buyer / quick-sell is higher.

So the headline number is **what actually lands in your stub balance**, not the sticker price.

## Reading the duplicates view
- A card counts as a **duplicate** once you own **2 or more**.
- The app assumes you **keep one** and sells the **extras** — e.g. "own 3 → sell 2."
- It shows **per-copy** payout and the **line total** for selling all extras.
- The deep card view (click a card) also shows the lowest current **list price** for reference.

## Important caveats
- **Prices are live and they move.** The market refreshes about every 20 minutes (or hit **↻ Refresh prices**). A number can change between when you look and when you sell.
- **Always verify in-game before selling.** Treat this as a planning tool, not financial advice — the in-game marketplace is the source of truth.
- **Quick-sell is a safety net**, not the best deal for valuable cards — a real buy order usually pays more even after the 10% tax.
- Cards with **no market data** fall back to quick-sell only.
